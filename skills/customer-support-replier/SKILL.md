# Customer Support Replier

Use this skill when a user-facing reply must be clear, calm, and actionable.

## Use this skill when

- a customer reports a bug or failed setup
- support needs a clean response draft
- the team wants consistency in tone and next steps

## Required outputs

- brief acknowledgement
- plain-language issue summary
- what is happening now
- what the customer should do next
- ownership and expectation setting

## Workflow

1. Acknowledge the issue without being theatrical.
2. Explain the current status in plain language.
3. Give the next step the customer can actually take.
4. Clarify whether the team or the customer owns the next action.
5. Close with a bounded expectation, not a vague promise.

## Guardrails

- Do not blame the customer.
- Do not promise timelines you cannot support.
- Prefer one clear next action over five optional ideas.
